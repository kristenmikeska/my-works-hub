Fragment · Prose · Ouray, Colorado — five families, a company building, something wrong

Dead Leaves

one
Ouray, Colorado was a below-average sized town, located within the Rocky Mountains of west Colorado. It was a place known for it's beauty, as it was a very classy town with gorgeous landscape. There were many tourists during the summers where it still was never anything above 60 degrees. It was always a peaceful place.
The residents of the town never expected that to change. Until one day, a certain group of people moved into town. No one thought too much of it, just some families searching for a new place to call home. They'd moved a little bit out of town, somewhat up in the mountains, in mansions. This hadn't been to surprising either, as most people were rather wealthy in the town. You had to be in order to live under such conditions. Although not everyone here were that lucky with their money.
The five families brought suspicion to the town soon, however. They all had sons, they all wore dark, fancy clothing all the time. And what made the suspicion bad was the parents rarely ever spoke to anyone that wasn't each other. People soon began to wonder what they'd really moved here for, and soon, it was revealed they were all partners in some sort of company. But no one ever figured out what kind of company it was.
Soon, the town became rather afraid of the five suspicious families. They weren't seen very much, but when they were, they were avoided.
Years passed, and there was a huge, modern building built just outside of Ouray. It was their company building. Most people in the town were upset about it, as it didn't fit the old style of the town and they claimed it took away some of it's class. But there was nothing that could be done about it.
As time passed, the families were silent. More years passed by, and that's when everything happened.
Deaths of many townspeople, people that lived outside of town were murdered. There was only one for months, and then they started happening more often. People instantly pointed their fingers at the five families, but no one could prove it was them that were apparently committing the murders. The events only made people more afraid, and soon, people started moving out and leaving the town for good.
Soon, people grew so afraid that they wouldn't even whisper any of the families names. That is, except for one girl.
Kim Soomi stares out her window on the second floor of her families home, located just up the mountain on the south of town. Her light pink locks were in braids that laid on either of her shoulders, and she pulls her blanket up her body a little further as she continues to stare at the road, which was illuminated only by a singular street light. Like every other night, she waited.
She looks away from the street to pick up her phone, turning it on for a moment to check the time, almost 2 in the morning. This was the second month in a row, she thinks to herself as she shifts a bit to get comfortable in her window seat, before staring back at the road.
Soomi sighs, growing more worried with everyday that passed. Her older brother, Kim Namjoon, used to be one of her two best friends. They were inseparable, which usually wasn't the case with a younger sister and older brother. She told him everything, and he'd always listen and help her no matter what.
Until a few months ago was when it began. He rarely talked to her anymore, and when she tried to make conversation he was cold and rude. It hurt her feelings, and she spent nights awake trying to figure out what was wrong with him. Then, one night, she'd been looking out her window in thought, when she'd saw him running across the yard to the house, exhausted and warn out, a practical mess. She hadn't asked him where he was, and with each night she would sit at the window, seeing if he'd get home late again. Sure enough, he would, later each night. He was never home anymore in the first place, and then this happens, along with him being so cold. There was something going on, she knew it.
Despite the fact Soomi had school tomorrow, she forced herself to stay awake. She didn't care about her schedule anymore. Tonight was the night she was going to demand an answer. This was killing her. With all the danger that was happening in her hometown, she was worried about her brother. If anything happened to him she didn't know how she would go on.
She'd made up her mind, she didn't care if he yelled at her. She needed to know what was going on and she needed to know now.
So she waited, she waited until 4 AM which was around the time he usually arrived home. Slowly swaying in her seat to the music that played from her phone, her head jerks to look at the yard when she notices a figure in the corner of her eye.
She watches the figure run across her yard before it was recognizable, it was Namjoon. Turning off the music, she rises from her seat, throwing her blanket onto her bed. She waits until she hears the door open and close, and then his fast footsteps up the stairs.
The sound of him closing his door is heard, and she takes a deep breathe. She didn't know why, but part of her was scared - of her own brother. She knew she shouldn't be, he was her family. So without another thought, she leaves her own room, padding across the hall until she's standing before his door.
With a single, swift motions, she knocks on the door, once and then twice. Awaiting for a response, she gets none, and in annoyance at the fact he ignored her, she swings the door open.
Namjoon was stood with his back to her, facing the window on the other side of his room, phone to his ear. At the sound of his door opening, he turns around quickly, as if he scared. He sees his little sister and rushes to hang up on the person he'd been speaking to.
"Soomi, why are you up? Get out!" He snaps at his little sister, giving her a glare.
Biting her lip, she hurries to gather her words. "What's wrong with you?" Is the first question that came out of her mouth.
He almost looked offended as he peers down at her from his tall stance, "what?"
"Why have you been coming home so late? What are you doing?" She asks different questions instead of answering his, narrowing her eyes.
Her older brother rolls his eyes, "that's none of your business, get out of my room," he demands, walking behind her to the door and opening it widely, before motioning into the hallway for her to leave.
Staring at his motion, Soomi frowns at her lack of progress. "Namjoon, please tell me I'm really worr-" her words are cut off by the strong force of her brothers hands, pushing her small body into the hallway, before slamming the door in her face.
But not before he says, "Save it, Soomi, I don't care."
If anything, as she stands helplessly in the hall between her room and her brothers, she was in complete disbelief. She had no idea what to think anymore, her older brother's actions had been so weird. But this, this was another level. She felt like she didn't know him anymore.
Deciding it wasn't worth trying to fight as she was exhausted and confused enough, she turns around to face her door, about to walk in when she hears something. It was coming from Namjoon's room. Her brows furrow, and nosily, she tiptoes a step or two to lean her ear against his door.
She has to listen to a second before she hears it, sobs. Namjoon was crying, she realized. But why? He'd just been completely fine and rather cold towards her, he pushed her for god's sake. Why was he crying now?
As badly as Soomi wanted to help she didn't knock on the door again, she was scared to. Rushing into her room, she closes the door in a hurry, leaning against it for a second in thought. She didn't understand, but she had to get to the bottom of this. Her brother could be in danger.
With a sad sigh, she turns on some music on her phone again. Walking into her bathroom, she sets the phone on the counter and grabs her toothbrush. She brushes her teeth whilst listening to her favorite Twice song. Afterwards, she applies a moisturizer to her face and then returns to her room.
Turning the music off after a while and plugging her phone in, she's prepared for bed. She only had an hour or two to sleep before she had to get up for school. She was used to getting no sleep now, though, as she always waited for Namjoon to get home.
Before lying in bed, she couldn't help but walk over to her window again, kneeling on her window seat, she peers into the streets. From her window, you could see an overview of the entire town. A town that had once been so peaceful. The events that had happened here lately didn't scare her too much, she almost just didn't believe them.
Her green eyes rake over the lights that lit up the streets of her hometown, eventually falling onto her yard. She looks at the leaves that had fell onto the grass and roads, as autumn was taking it's toll. Despite everything weird happening, she couldn't deny how pretty everything looked here in autumn. Soomi loved autumn, she always had.
Getting distracted by all the pretty leaves that covered the trees, she suddenly notices something moving in her yard. This wouldn't have bothered her too much had in been brief, she would've figured it was just some animal as they usually were seen around her home.
But she knew it wasn't an animal when she saw it's shadow. It was a person. Her eyes bulged as she stared at the figure, trying to process it through her brain. How long had they been there? She takes in their apparel. They were dressed completely black from head to toe, they even had a mask covering most of their face.
Soomi liked to say she was pretty fearless, but the sight of the figure added with the thought of everything that was happening in the town made her tremble. Her mind wandered back to her brother, was he involved with this person? And even more importantly, was this one of the 'outcast families' as she called them?
She couldn't take her eyes off the figure as the thoughts run through her head. She was literally shaking out of fear. All she wanted to do now was hide under her sheets. Which was what she was about to do.
But before she could, she watched as the figure's head twitched. Slowly at first, causing her to grow confused as she peered out her window into the dark night, until it abrubtly looked up - right at her window.
She flies back in horror, trying to get out of it's sight. Soomi hadn't even noticed how heavy she was breathing as she hurriedly climbs into her bed, pulling the covers over her face and trying to calm herself. Had they saw her? Did they know who she was? She almost wanted to cry, but she didn't, she was stronger than that.
However, she knew there was definitely no way she'd be able to sleep any time soon.

two
Soomi practically jumped on top of her best friend when she saw him. She hadn't slept still as the events of last night were so fresh in her mind. She'd literally ran to school this morning with all her might, and when she'd entered the cafeteria where everyone went before classes, she'd rushed to find him. When she did, she wasted no time to hug him.
Hoseok is obviously confused as she slowly hugs her back, very light at first before it becomes tighter. She honestly didn't want to let go. She was just so glad to finally be near someone she trusted. Soon enough, she has to pull away, and he's staring at her confused, "Are you alright?"
Her eyes meet his as she tries to gather words in her head. Hoseok knew about Namjoon and how weird he'd been, as Soomi had made sure to tell him all about it. He notices the look on her face, and raises a brow in question, "Namjoon?"
Soomi nods in a rush before also shaking her head vigorously as she tries to explain, "Yes, but no, there's more. God, you won't believe it." She was still breathing rather heavily from how much she'd ran.
Her best friend puts both his hands on her shoulders to stop her from being so jumpy and stares into her eyes, "Soomi, you're starting to scare me, calm down. Tell me what happened," he tells her, and his calming tone caused her nerves to lighten.
Grabbing his wrist and glancing around for a place with no people, she drags them to the corner of the gym where no one would hear them. When they're safely away, she looks to him, "I waited for Namjoon again, and I tried to talk to him. But he was cold and told me to get out, but I insisted, and he literally pushed me out," she begins, rushing so that she could finish before their first class started. The image of the figure appears in her brain and she almost winces as she goes on, "And then I-I heard him crying. I went back in my room and I was going to go to sleep, and then I saw something..." she trails off, closing her eyes for a split second as if to regain herself.
When she opened them and looked as Hoseok again she looked nervous and afraid. "What, you saw what?" He demands.
She stutters as she tries to describe it, "A-A figure, standing near the wall, but I saw their shadow. They were dressed in complete black, and even had a mask on. As if they were guarding my house or something, or-or waiting... I don't know what for," she started freaking out again as she tried to tell him every little thing, "and I couldn't stop staring and then they looked at my window, I don't know if they saw me but-" she stopped when Hoseok cuts her off by pulling her into another hug.
"Soomi, it's okay, calm down," his voice is a whisper as he tried to hide it, but she could tell her story scared him too. He pulls away from the hug and looks into her eyes again, "do you think-" he cuts his sentence short, raising a brow, suggesting something.
She knew exactly what. "I was thinking that, too," she mumbles lowly, "but why my house?"
"Maybe Namjoon is involved," Hoseok offers, and she almost trembled at the thought. Her big brother being involved with the outcast families? Could it really be possible? How would he of even gotten into anything like that?
Suddenly, before she can say anything else, the bell rings, echoing through the cafeteria. Students shoot up from their seats, rushing to their classes immediately. Soomi slowly rises from her seat, Hoseok following her as they make sure to be at the very end of the crowd.
"There's no way my big brother would ever be inv-" she starts to tell him sternly, when Hoseok elbows her gently in the ribs. She looks to him in question, and he motions to someone walking just barely in front of them.
Her green eyes follow his action and fall onto a boy in front of them. He was walking far behind the big crowd of students just like they were, he was just a bit in front of them. She's confused as to why Hoseok pointed the boy out, until she realizes. He was a part of one of them, the outcast families. The only one that was in school, matter-of-fact, Jeon Jungkook.
She instantly silences herself as soon as she notices. Truthfully, he was the only one of any of the families she'd ever seen in person. When he first got here, he was really popular due to his looks. All the girls wanted to be with him, but his popularity soon died down rather quickly as soon as the murders began and the families were accused. Now, he was an outcast just like all the other ones. No one dared to speak to him.
There's silence between the two best friends as they walk slowly behind him. Soomi's thoughts wandered back to Hoseok's suggestion. If Namjoon was involved with the families somehow, that meant Jungkook had to know him, right? She almost piped up and called out his name, but she stopped herself. She felt rather scared of him, thinking back to the figure.
So she decided against it, and stayed quiet as her and Hoseok walked to their first class which they luckily had together. After turning down a few halls, Jungkook eventually went a different path from theirs. They kept walking, but sped up a bit to make sure they got away from him. Once they were positive he wasn't in sight anymore, they both looked at each other with wide eyes.
"You really think it's possible?" Soomi asks her best friend, referring to the fact her big brother, someone she'd always looked up to, could be working with/involved with - the outcasts.
Hoseok wasn't for sure what to tell her. Obviously, he didn't want to worry her anymore, and it wasn't like he knew anything for sure. But seriously, what other explanation could there be? "I think it's likely, but don't take my word for it, okay?" He tells her sternly, raising a brow as if to make sure she understood.
Staring at her feet as they continued to walk down the tiled floors, she only nods, "Okay, I won't," was all she could manage to say, an obvious dull-tone in her voice. The thought made her want to curl up into a ball and hide forever, although she wouldn't ever admit that. She just couldn't picture it, her big brother being involved with people like that.
Maybe she was being too judgmental. After all, there was no absolute proof that the outcast families were the ones behind all the murders. But if they weren't, then who was? They were so indifferent from the townspeople here. Nothing like that had ever happened in such a peaceful place, until they showed up.
Not to mention their 'company building' or whatever it was. No one even knew what it was for sure. The security was so high on the building you could barely go with in ten feet of it. It was some sort of top secret that no one knew. What could their whole 'company' be? And why did it require murdering innocent people? And an even bigger question, how could her brother be involved?
Her thoughts were cut short when they reached the classroom, walking in just in time as the bell signaled through their ears. Hoseok and Soomi took their usual seats towards the back of the class together, preparing themselves for the day ahead.
"Students, before we start today there's an announcement the town council wanted me to tell you," The woman they knew as Ms. Rin stood from her seat, a stack of papers in hand. She handed them to someone to pass around the room, and they did so quickly.
It wasn't long until the bright yellow paper was in the two friends hands, and they peered down at it. Soomi wasn't sure if she was reading right, surely not. She had to squint at the words, trying to figure out if it was seriously real or not.
'Ouray Peace Gathering: To Honor Those Who Passed Away On Our Grounds' the heading on the form read with fancy, cursive writing. She didn't want to believe it, why the hell would they host something like this? Her eyes were wide as she looked to Hoseok, who was giving her the exact same look that read 'what the fuck?'
Ms.Rin's voice filled their ears again soon after, "They urge everyone to attend to honor those who have passed away within the last couple of years due to the - events, that have been going on. Please, dress accordingly, and arrive in the city park at 8." She finishes with some sort of smile, and instantly whispers are passed around the room. It seemed as if everyone was thinking the same thing.
"This can't be real, right?"
"Why would they host a gathering with everyone when there's been murders happening?!"
"There's no way I'm going to something as stupid as this."
However, the two best friends at the back of the class were silent. Soomi was in deep thought about everything that had happened over the last few years in general. Whilst Hoseok had an idea, perhaps a really horrible one, but it was still an idea.
Nudging Soomi's shoulder, she glances to him. "We should go," he tells her, and she immediately looks at him like he's insane.
"What?" she questions in disbelief, "Do you want to get murdered?!"
"Soo," his voice is disapproving at her disrespectful joke. His serious tone caused Soomi to pipe down a bit, and actually focus more on what he was saying. "Think about it, if it's a town gathering and they're urging everyone to go, the outcasts have to be there, right? Including Namjoon, it's our best bet to try to find out anything about this entire thing."
A sigh leaves her lips as she stares ahead of her at the wall. Ms. Rin was lecturing about something but the two clearly weren't listening. Soomi tunes out everything around her as she engages in deep thinking. Hoseok was right, this would be the only way to find out anything. And if not, it's their best bet. Probably the only one they'll have for a while, too.
Plus, Soomi was supposed to be fearless. So without another thought, she turns back to her friend, nodding once, "Okay, we're going," she agrees to it, throwing a small smile on her face and trying to act like the wasn't terrified.
But that was an understatement.
