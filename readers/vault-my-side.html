01:00 | persistent friends & raised questions
You'd think she'd be used to the long walk to school everyday, but still, she found her entire body begging for her to just collapse on the damp sidewalk and sleep for the rest of her life. Her feet felt like they were bleeding as she adjusted the skirt of her school uniform, lazily slumping down much like a bum as she splashed through the puddles forming on the sidewalks, making her way through the large entrance gates of one the disgusting horror private schools were.
She got a few looks from students from how dead she probably looked, and it wasn't like the way she was walking like a zombie was helping her either. She was only just about a singular foot into the gates of the school when there was the sound of a car honking behind her, which wouldn't of made her turn around until she heard her name being called followed by such an obnoxious sound.
Her exhausted body turned in the humid air, her eyes squinting as she noticed the familiar boy climbing out of the vehicle, dressed in his own yellow uniform. The window of the vehicle he'd gotten out of had been rolled down, where the tall boy in the drivers seat and grinned, waving at her as he yelled, "Mijoo, don't tell me you walked to school today?!"
Jesus, she could only think at the fact he was literally yelling a conversation across the entrance of the school from his car. With a sigh, she walked towards the broken down, old 2006 Toyota, a chuckling Hyunjin following behind her as he'd caught up to where she was by now.
She'd waited until she was standing by the vehicle, her hands holding the part of the door where the window would otherwise be located as she leaned inside the car a bit to reply to his previous statement, "Channie, I always walk to school."
The look on his face was that of an upset father, as he scoffed in astonishment. "With this weather? I swear - Changbin seriously needs to learn how to take care of his beloved little sister."
Chuckling a bit, she heard Hyunjin making noises of disgust from his older brother's words, making her chuckle turn into a laugh as she looked back to the eldest, "uh, I think he does fine - I don't need him practically choking me like you do to poor Hyunjinnie."
Next thing you knew, Chan was rolling his eyes, obviously not taking her words much to heart since she was joking. Although there was some truth to it.
"What're siblings for?" he only hummed sarcastically, knowing well of how she'd only begin to list the ninety things her and Changbin did to each other along the lists of insults and pranks. Before she could open her mouth though, he'd raised a hand from the steering wheel to point a finger at her, "I'll give you a ride to school tomorrow since Changbin's such a lazy fuck. Be ready at 7:15 sharp."
Surprised but also not from the sudden demand, her green eyes widened a bit. Mijoo should've already known there was nothing she would be able to do to change Chan's mind, as he was just that much of a dad.
This didn't mean she wasn't going to try. "It's okay, I like walking."
He gave her a clear look of disbelief, and it was then she cursed the fact he'd known her for so long.
"Alright," she put her hands up in defense, tired of him giving her that scary look. It made you feel like you had to be the dumbest person to walk the earth, and it wasn't something she was looking to endure any longer, "I'll be ready."
Hyunjin had laid his head on her shoulder from behind her, seeming to grow tired of waiting for his friend to stop talking to his brother. "She agreed, hyung, now we need to you know - actually go to fucking school."
Chan sent a hard glare right into his soul, "watch your language, first of all," Hyunjin only let out a deep sigh of annoyance, "and second of all, you kids have a good day." Chan said those words like the two of them were in grade school, and it was funny to her as she could only giggle a bit. Letting go of the car, she offered a wave to her brother's friend as he drove away.
A sigh was heard from Hyunjin as the two turned back to walk into the gates of the school finally. She looked up to him in question, only for him to already be speaking, "he really bothers me sometimes."
She only shrugs, "he has good intentions." But you really couldn't disagree.
The walk to the cafeteria wasn't a long one. It was like every other day at school, girls would throw themselves and melt over Hyunjin, and she would laugh at their attempts because he was always so uninterested. She'd known him for so long, and never once had he found himself caring for a girl. It didn't make sense, not in the slightest now that they were older. You'd think he'd care about finding a girlfriend, especially in their last years of high school.
With him, and his friends, being so widely popular among the girl's wishes in their school, a lot of them weren't fans of her. This was why she found herself having so few of friends through out the years of high school.
It wasn't like that mattered to her, anyway. As long as she had those few friends, what else would she need?
This is why she held her head high, pretending to laugh at a really lame joke Hyunjin was telling her as they entered the cafeteria doors. The loud chatter going on across the large room filled their ears, but she still heard the pout not only evident on his face but from his voice as he whined, "it was funny, you don't have to give me that fake laugh."
Holding in the urge to not actually laugh at his facial expression that was that of a child, she shrugged, "my bad, Hyunjinnie," before raising a question as they stopped in the middle of the cafeteria, "you going to sit with your friends?"
Adjusting his jacket and the strap of his backpack on his shoulder, the dark haired boy ran a hand through his locks before nodding slowly, "yeah, but why don't you just come sit with us, too? You can just talk to Felix," he sent her that famous grin of his, trying his hardest to convince her. He did this a lot, as all Hyunjin had ever seemed to want was for her to be friends with his friends (except for a certain someone.) The only one she ever became friends with was Felix, but she'd always felt too iffy around the rest of them.
Hyunjin knew being around them was about of her comfort zone, but he didn't want it to be. The thing about it was that as long as she avoided them, it'd never be fixed. It was the reason he never quit bothering her about it every day.
There was a long pause of her not saying her usual fuck no. In confusion as to why she hadn't said anything yet, Hyunjin peered down at her from his tall height, only to see her eyes had found their way somewhere else across the cafeteria. He tried to figure out what she was looking at, before she had whipped her emerald eyes back to him.
"Actually, Felix is sitting with Eden," she hummed matter-of-factly. His eyes had found the boy she had just mentioned, which was now clearly what she had been looking at, only to see he wasn't sitting with his friends like Hyunjin had just said - but with Mijoo's other best friend.
In that moment, he wished it was possible to hit someone from across the other side of the room. Groaning in frustration, he diverted his gaze from the boy, "I swear, he's fucking obsessed with you."
Mijoo was chuckling as she shook her head at his accusation, "don't be silly, but looks like there's no reason for me to sit with your friends, now."
He frowned, walking along at her side as she was already making her way toward the table. Hyunjin noted her eyes that were locked onto Felix as he endlessly tried one last time, "uh, yes there is - I'm there."
If only you really knew, he could only think as she was already throwing her book bag onto the seat beside Felix. Mijoo had fell into the plastic seat at the table before looking back up to a defeated Hyunjin, "sorry, but I think I choose Felix and Eden over you."
"Mijoo-ah, what're you guys on about this time?" Eden had overheard the conversation, raising a brow from where she'd been focused on her phone.
Felix had looked from Hyunjin's pouty expression, to the brunette sitting next to him, before concluding, "I'm assuming it's about how you want her to meet the guys." He was well aware of how Hyunjin endlessly tried to get Mijoo to be friends with the others. It didn't matter much to him, as it was clear she wasn't interested in making anymore friends - and not to mention, it was dangerous for her. He wasn't quite sure what Hyunjin's obsession was with it, he guessed he just wanted to be able to hang out with her more since they'd always been so close.
Hyunjin had opened his mouth to speak from where he was standing, but Mijoo beat him to it as she looked to Felix and Eden, "that's exactly what it's about."
"Just let it go," Felix hummed to the boy, who only glared at him.
A final sigh leaving his lips, the dark haired teenager had pointed an accusing finger at the blonde, "you're a lucky bitch, Felix," before he'd turned on his heel, making his way towards the table where his friends were located at.
Mijoo and Eden had stared at him in confusion as he walked away like some sort of sass queen, strutting as if he was on a run way. She would've laughed if it was any other day, but Hyunjin's words to Felix had left her confused. What's he talking about? How did him begging me to sit with his friends turn into something about Felix?
It made no sense to the two girls, but Felix knew exactly what his friend had been talking about.
Eager to change the subject because he knew well enough one of them was about to bring it up if he didn't, he glanced to the brunette beside him, "you know, the other's aren't all that bad."
Eden had been leaning her chin in the palm of her hand as she looked to the two, sitting across from them both. Her blue eyes fell on Felix, curious of what he just said as luckily it took their minds off of Hyunjin's last words, "you say that like there's someone that is bad."
He only shrugs, not denying the statement, "that's why you don't talk to them and they don't talk to you."
Mijoo was confused by the current conversation that was happening. She was too much of a naive, innocent girl to really understand just what they were specifically speaking of. Her green eyes blinked a few times as she looks back and forth at the two, "wait - who are we even talking about?"
Eden shrugged much like Felix just had, "I don't know," she looks back to the blonde, who seemed nervous of the current conversation, "so, who's so bad in you and Hyunjin's friend group, Felix?"
You could tell he was extremely uncomfortable with everything that was happening. He'd been caught in a sticky spot and wasn't quite sure how to get out of it without lighting something on fire.
Biting his lip, his brown eyes looked everywhere but the two girls staring into his soul. His brain was working at mach speeds as he tried to think of something to say - when it finally hit him.
"Why's it matter if you're not going to meet them, anyway?"
Eden had pursed her lips, realizing his words were suspicious but also valid. Mijoo frowned a bit at how he had answered their question with another question, turning away as she knew it was directed at her.
"I guess it doesn't."

02:00 | rude awakening
Never had Mijoo spoken to any of Hyunjin and Felix's friends. Of course, she'd seen them many times - or most of them - but never did she offer them a word despite their mutual friends. Perhaps they were actually nice people, but after what Felix had said earlier - it had her feeling even more on edge about them than usual.
She had trust issues, she always had. How could you not after the trauma's you'd been put through? This was a huge reason she worried about meeting them all, she just didn't know them. Of course, she trusted Hyunjin - but what if he didn't know them as well as he thinks he does? There were too many questions that built into her brain, creating a wall of worry that she was too weak to begin to climb over.
It was during her last period of the day that she found herself feeling more exhausted than she had been this morning. She was sure Chan wouldn't mind giving her a ride home if he comes to pick up Hyunjin, and hopefully he would. Otherwise, her best bet would be calling Changbin and begging him to actually pick her up for once since she didn't have a license nor car. This was because Mijoo knew for sure she didn't have the energy to walk home, not today.
A long yawn leaves her lips as she resists the urge to lay her head down on the desk, knowing that if she does she'll pass out. The last thing she wanted was to fall asleep in last period, then end up waking up after everyone leaves and the doors are locked. Mijoo knew damn well her rude teacher wouldn't care.
Although she knew this, her entire body seemed to be screaming at her. I really need to fix my sleeping schedule, she pouted, burying her face in her hands and rubbing her eyes as she tries to stay awake. The days almost over, Mijoo, she gives herself some reassuring words mentally, but this didn't help her exhaustion levels.
She hadn't even taken any of the notes for her literature class due to how she could barely even think. I can just ask Hyunjin or Felix, she thought, although it wasn't likely they took them either whether they were half asleep or wide awake.
Feeling dead and hopeless, it only became harder to fight the urge to sleep. Her brain shutting down more with each second that passed on the clock that hung on the wall across the room, her entire mind was too weak to resist it as her head fell onto the desk, buried in her arms, and her green eyes were forced shut.

Is he who Felix was speaking of?
