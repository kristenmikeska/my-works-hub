At least, all of them except for one.

Outside, the weather was a gloomy grey, the clouds angered in the sky as they cried out their emotions from up above. From her brunette locks that fell in waves down her back, to her eyes that glowed an emerald green under the light. She was tiny to say the least, and she'd been gawking at him like she saw some sort of ghost.

She'd jumped a bit again, finally diverting her eyes from how they'd been staring into his soul. Frantically, she started gathering her things laid out on the desk, "h-hi, is this your seat? I'm sorry."
When she spoke, he could very much tell she wasn't used to using Korean. She really must've moved here recently, he thought as he watched her start shoving her things in the bag that was hanging off the edge of the chair.
Remembering how she was raised in America, he tried to remember the amounts of English he'd learned from his own time spent there with his parents. Since she seemed rather anxious from his presence or maybe the sudden pressure, perhaps it would ease her.
"It's okay, you can sit there," he spoke as clearly as he could manage in English. it caused her eyes to whip back to him, where she blinked a few times, taking in what he said to her.
Stopping her movements, she turned back to him to speak back in the same language, her tone much clearer and not shaky anymore, "are you sure? This is your seat, after all. I'm sorry that nobody told me."
Waving a hand in dismissal, he offered her a small grin, "don't worry about it," she gave him a sympathetic look, seeming to feel bad about something so small, "plus, my friend Felix here has been excited to meet you," he motioned to Felix, who had been talking to someone else in the room.

"He's an exchange student as well," Jisung explained, to which she hummed a noise of understanding.
His words had seemed to excite her, however, "really? I'm glad I'm not alone!"
