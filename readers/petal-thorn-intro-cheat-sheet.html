What is Petal & Thorn?

"Petal & Thorn" is a high-stakes, emotional crossover fanfiction that blends the glossy, high-pressure world of K-pop idol culture with the competitive intensity of professional eSports (Overwatch League). It follows Solar (an original character, facecard is Karina from Aespa/reader insert portrayed as a member of the girl group Le Sserafim), who leads a double life: by day, she is a polished idol; by night, she is a top-tier DPS player for the San Francisco Shock. 


The narrative deconstructs the idea of perfection, exploring the cracks behind the "idol mask." It is a story about trauma, identity, and the desperate need to be "seen" for who you really are, rather than the image you project. 


The story is angtsy, a romantic melodrama, idolverse with psychological horror aspects. The writing is atmospheric, internal, descriptive. Each chapter is over 2000 words. It is meant to be spicy, hot, and eccentric. 


The story has a lot of plotlines and in-depth character backgrounds that are slowly becoming more prevalent and clear as the book progresses. The characters are each true to their personalities and unique to themselves. 


The Timeline Broken Down

Age 12 — Started playing Overwatch. Ranked matches at home, scabbed knees, old headset, Chaerin yelling from the kitchen to do her homework. Gaming was an escape, not a career plan yet.

Age 15 — Auditioned for HYBE. Trembling fingers on a mic. This is the open audition — she walked in and tried. It likely took a year or two of training before debut, putting debut somewhere around 17–18.

Age 16 — The photo that Jihoon weaponizes. She's at one of his parties — pale, glassy-eyed, plastic cup in hand. His grooming was in full effect at this point: his whispers in her ear, "no one knows you like I do," his hand tightening on her wrist when she hesitated, something bitter pressed into her palm. This is the darkest period.

Age 17 — Crying in the bathroom after Jihoon ghosted her. This means the relationship — or whatever it was — had been ongoing from at least 16 into 17. He didn't just hurt her and disappear; she was entangled enough that when he pulled away, it broke her. She was also likely already a trainee at this point, which means the Jihoon years and the HYBE trainee years overlapped. He knew her during training. He saw her building something and was simultaneously tearing her down.

Age 18 (implied) — Debut with Le Sserafim. The text doesn't state this explicitly, but the math between 15 (audition) and 20 (present) with a trainee period of 2–3 years places debut around 17–18. The fact that she's described as a fully established idol by the present timeline supports this.

Age 19 — "Clicking 'accept match' while the rest of the dorm slept." She was already a debuted idol living in the Le Sserafim dorm. This is when she was grinding ranked Overwatch in secret — building her VOD library, climbing to Top 500, not yet scouted. This is the hidden year. Nobody knew. She was doing it alone, in the dark, between schedules.

Age 20 (present) — The story's "now." She has been scouted by Crusty, joined the Shock, won the 2025 OWL Grand Finals, and is now navigating the HYBE Invitational while Jihoon and Danielle are closing in.

The Jihoon Overlap — What It Means for Your Flashbacks
The most narratively rich and disturbing detail is that Jihoon and HYBE coexisted in Solar's life simultaneously. She was training to be an idol at 15, and by 16 she was already in Jihoon's orbit. That means:

  • He may have found her because she was a trainee — someone with ambition, something to lose, someone who could be controlled.
  • The years she was working the hardest toward her dream were the same years he was chipping away at her sense of self.
  • When he ghosted her at 17, she was still a trainee — she had to pick herself back up, go back into the HYBE building, and keep going. Every practice room she walked into after that was walked into alone.

The photo he now holds as leverage — 16-year-old Solar, glassy-eyed, at his party — was taken during what should have been her most formative years as a trainee. He essentially photographed proof that she was breaking during the time she was supposed to be becoming someone.

What the Text Leaves Deliberately Vague (For You to Define)
These are the gaps the existing text doesn't resolve, which you'll need to decide for your rewrite:

  • How did Solar meet Jihoon? Through Chaerin (who had her own chaos at this time)? At an industry-adjacent event? Through mutual friends of older trainees? The text doesn't say.
  • How long was the Jihoon entanglement? The text confirms 16 (grooming/photo) and 17 (ghosted/abandoned). But did it start earlier at 14–15? Was it one concentrated year or a slow creep?
  • What exactly happened at the party? The "bitter taste of escape pressed into her palm" strongly implies a substance. Whether it was given willingly, coerced, or slipped to her without consent is left ambiguous — and that ambiguity is intentional in the original, but you'll need to decide how explicit to be in the rewrite.
  • Did Jaehyun know at the time? The text implies he recognized the photo immediately and knew what night it was from. That means he either knew while it was happening and did nothing, or found out after. Either answer is heavy.
  • The Shock scouting timing — the text says the scouting DM arrived "last week" relative to when Solar tells Yunjin, and the audition was on a Sunday night. It feels very recent — like weeks before the story's present-day action begins. So the double life is newly formed when the story opens.

Suggested Flashback Architecture for Your Rewrite
Since you want to scatter past moments through the present narrative, here's a natural structure based on what exists:

Early fragments (ages 12–13): The headset. The scabbed knees. The ranked queue running while homework sat unfinished. These are the before — the versions of Solar who was just a kid finding something that felt like power. Warm, nostalgic, uncomplicated.

The HYBE audition (age 15): The trembling fingers on the mic. This is the moment she chose the idol path deliberately. It can be shot through with ambivalence — she wanted it and was terrified of it.

The Jihoon years (ages 16–17): The darkest material. Best served in pieces — a party, a hand on a wrist, a whispered lie, a ghosted message, a morning after something she couldn't name. These should feel suffocating and slow, not dramatic. Mundane cruelty is scarier than big cruelty.

The dorm nights at 19: Grinding ranked alone while her members slept. These are the reclamation fragments — the part where she quietly started building something back. The gaming chair in the dark as an act of defiance.

Each cluster of flashbacks escalates as Jihoon and Danielle escalate in the present — so the reader is literally piecing her together as she's being taken apart.

The Chaerin-rehab / Yein-college timing detail is actually useful. If Yein is around 17–18 in 2025 and just started college, and Chaerin's rehab stint and Yein's college enrollment are both described as events that mark the last time the family was all together — that means the family fractured and reunited within the last year or two, which lines up cleanly with the story's present tension.

Why this matters for flashbacks:

  • During Solar's Jihoon years (age 16–17, so 2021–2022), Yein would have been around 13–14 — young enough to be sheltered from it, which explains why she's described as the "quiet reason" rather than someone with firsthand knowledge of what happened
  • Chaerin (around 16–17 when Solar was 15) would have been going through her own spiral at almost exactly the same time Solar was being groomed — which means the two sisters were both drowning simultaneously and couldn't save each other
  • Jaehyun (around 23–24 during Solar's worst years) was already in his shadowy adult life — old enough to have resources and information, which makes his inaction during that period even more complicated and damning

That last point is particularly rich for your rewrite. Jaehyun knew the Lee family's secrets well enough to recognize the photo immediately — meaning he was aware of what was happening to Solar at 16 and made a strategic choice to stay silent rather than intervene visibly. That's the wound beneath his relationship with her.
