"Let's go, guess we'll give her some more time and see if she decides to talk."

A slam echoes through the empty, sad room as the heavy door is slammed shut. 

Once again, she was alone. 

Alone.

Staring. 

It was all she had been doing for an amount of time she was unaware of. How much time had passed? It could've been just a few days, it could've been months, even a year. 

Solar didn't know. She didn't know because she hadn't processed what was happening yet, she was still lost in her head, in her own world where everything was normal, where everything was peaceful. Her own world where nothing bad ever happened, and everyone was happy. 

Most of the time, she was safe in that world in her mind. Though, sometimes, it could creep into her thoughts. Memories would flash, vivid images with vulgar portrayals would surface in her brain. And she would grimace, trying to push them away, trying to tell herself those were fake. 

They weren't fake. 

She was crying.

It was so sudden, so unexpected, until she felt the wet tear drops hit her hands. A part of her almost didn't know why she was crying, because a part of her was so numb and disconnected that she could almost forget. 

She cried, and cried, until the door had been opened again with a creek. 

"Miss Solar," the voice of the woman entered her ears. This time she was alone, without the man. Her heeled shoes click-clack against the concrete floor of the room. Clearing her throat, she scoots the chair out and sits down. 

A word didn't leave the girl's lips. She just sat there, crying, head hanging low, staring at the ground. It almost looked as if she was mumbling to herself, words that were so slight, so quiet, no one could quite make them out. 

"Gone... killer... gunshot... friends..."

In fact, I know you're not," she affirms, "that's why I need you to help me help you, alright? Tell me what happened that day." 

Silence.

More silence.

Until the girl shifted a bit in the metal chair, handcuffs clinging against it and echoing through the walls. 

"He wanted me dead..." she mutters out weakly. Her matted, unkept brown hair fell in her face as her eyes teared up again, "he wanted me dead my whole life. They wanted me dead. They wanted me dead so bad they would do anything... people died... it was my fault... I created this mess by... being alive... I should have died... I was supposed to die..." 

She was sobbing again, choking on her words until she was hyperventilating. The detective slides a box of tissues her way, pulling one out and holding it out to the girl. All she could do was watch, watch before her eyes one of the world's most beloved celebrities crumbling before her. 

It had been that way for a while. Solar's condition wasn't getting better.

No words left the woman's mouth. Solar just laughed, she kept laughing and laughing. Sitting there, in that same metal chair, giggling away like a psychopath.

She was losing herself, or maybe she was lost all along. 

"Okay, Solar, I just want you to know that I tried to help you," she finally exhales, rising to her feet again, "if you ever want to talk, just let me know. I'll come see you." 

Then she left again, back to her casual free life.
