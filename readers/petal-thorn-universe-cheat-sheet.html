SOLAR — THE JIHOON ENTANGLEMENT (Ages 15–17)

How It Begins

  • Chaerin is already party-hopping and using drugs around this time (see Chaerin's timeline below)
  • Chaerin brings Solar to one of Jihoon's parties — not knowing who throws them, just going for the scene
  • Chaerin is chaotic and impulsive during this period; dragging her 15-year-old sister to a party is consistent with who she is at this stage
  • Jihoon sees Solar and fixates on her immediately

The Entanglement (Ages 15–17, approx. 2020–2022)

  • Spans roughly two years — not one dramatic event, but a slow, suffocating pattern
  • Solar attends multiple parties over this period, not just one
  • Jihoon is calculated from the start: whispers, manufactured intimacy, "no one knows you like I do," isolation tactics
  • The relationship is coercive — he made her feel chosen, then used that feeling as control
  • The photo that is now being weaponized: taken at the worst/darkest of these parties (age 16, 2021). Solar was likely drugged without consent. This is the most traumatic event of the entanglement — deeply dark, to be handled carefully but with weight when written
  • Solar was a minor and a HYBE trainee throughout this entire period — he had access to someone with everything to lose

How It Ends

  • Jaehyun finally becomes aware of what's happening (likely too late — he was preoccupied with his own pressures and early involvement in "the shadows")
  • Jaehyun intervenes — likely around Solar's debut preparation period, which gives him leverage and urgency
  • Solar debuting with Le Sserafim is also a key factor: the new support system (LSF girls), the new identity, the structure of dorm life all create distance
  • Solar cuts off contact around age 17–18
  • Jihoon never fully accepted this — in the present story, he still texts her "you texted me, remember?" referring to one weak moment she had

Jaehyun's Role

  • Was NOT closely watching during the worst of it — he was dealing with parental pressure and beginning his own shadowy dealings
  • Realizes too late — this is the source of his guilt, which manifests as cold control in the present
  • His intervention when he does act is effective but not loving — it's strategic, not emotional
  • He recognized the leaked party photo immediately in the present, meaning he knows exactly what happened that night. Whether he knew in real time or pieced it together after is a decision for the rewrite (see Open Questions)

    • A licensing/loaned-player type arrangement is brokered
  • The HYBE meeting where executives tell her to "be smart" and lay down rules suggests they found out and reluctantly greenlit it with conditions
  • This needs to be fully mapped before writing the scouting arc

First Meeting with the Team (Voice Call)

  • Solar meets the Shock boys for the first time over voice — Super screaming, Smurf being wholesome, Nero immediately causing chaos, Glister calling her unnie
  • The public speculates for a period about who the mystery girl on the Shock roster is, before it's revealed to be Solar from LE SSERAFIM

CHAERIN'S SPIRAL — TIMELINE (To Be Fleshed Out)

What We Know from the Text

  • A leaked photo of Chaerin from "three years ago" (so ~2022) shows her at a club, bleached and uneven hair, eyeliner like armor, drink in hand
  • She had a "last rehab stint" — the family dinner scene references it as a past event, meaning she's currently recovered
  • She is now sharp, protective, present — the version of her in the present story is post-recovery

Proposed Framework (To Confirm)

  • ~2019–2020 (age ~19–20): Chaerin begins partying heavily; substance use begins. She's young, impulsive, seeking escape — the specific trigger is TBD (parental pressure? industry rejection? general chaos?)
  • 2020 (Solar is 15): Chaerin is already embedded in the party scene when she brings Solar to Jihoon's party. She is not malicious — she's reckless and thinks she's giving her little sister a fun experience. She does not know Jihoon or who throws the parties
  • 2020–2022: Chaerin's spiral deepens at the same time Solar's entanglement with Jihoon worsens. Both sisters are drowning simultaneously and cannot save each other
  • ~2022 (age ~21–22): The leaked club photo is from this era — this is peak spiral
  • ~2022–2023: Jaehyun intervenes (same period he acts on Solar's situation). Chaerin goes to rehab
  • Present (2025): Recovered. The photo being leaked by Danielle is a direct attack on the Lee family — Chaerin is now a target because she's Solar's sister and because her past is weaponizable

Open Questions for Chaerin

  • What specifically triggered her drug use? (Parental pressure, industry rejection, heartbreak, general chaos?)
  • What is her substance of choice? (Matters for how the spiral is written)
  • Does she know, now in the present, the full extent of what happened to Solar at Jihoon's parties? When did she find out?
  • How guilty does she feel about being the one who brought Solar into that world?


JUNGWON — BACKSTORY (The Circle)

His Father

  • Father was wealthy but volatile — businessman, drinking problem, violent tendencies
  • Mother absent (left early or passed away) — this absence made him vulnerable
  • Abuse was both physical and psychological — constant degradation, told he was worthless, intermittently kicked out for hours or days at a time

The Streets (Age ~13–15)

  • When kicked out, young Jungwon had nowhere to go
  • Fell in with people operating in Seoul's underground — illegal fighting circuits tied to gambling rings
  • He was young, angry, and good at it — his rage made him fearless
  • The people running these circuits gave him belonging and purpose when his father gave him nothing

The Circle

  • A criminal organization — enforcers, debt collectors, people who make problems disappear
  • Took Jungwon in when he was at his lowest; offered twisted family and brotherhood
  • Full membership required proof of loyalty

The Ultimatum & What He Did

  • The Circle told him: "Your father is a liability. He talks too much when he drinks. He owes money to people who don't forgive debts. If you want to be one of us, you handle it."
  • Jungwon's father died — most likely staged as an accident, or occurred during a violent confrontation that Jungwon didn't stop
  • He carries this weight. It is the wound beneath his controlled fury
  • After the father's death, something in him cracked. He realized what he was becoming and ran from the organization

Getting Out

  • Leaving The Circle is dangerous — they don't release people
  • Jungwon leveraged information he'd collected on the organization to make himself too risky to pursue
  • Someone helped him disappear — changed his name legally, buried every connection, became someone new
  • Was accepted as a HYBE trainee; built a new life entirely

The Circle in the Present Story

  • Jihoon discovers Jungwon's past through a contact ("Min") and purchases The Circle's complete file on Jungwon
  • The Circle is now being used by Jihoon: surveillance on both Solar and Jungwon, psychological pressure, manufactured paranoia
  • Jihoon's goal: keep them separated, break Jungwon, isolate Solar
  • Jihoon's line: "I don't need him dead. I need him broken."
  • Jungwon is aware The Circle exists and fears what they could expose

Storytelling Decision: When to Reveal

  • The Circle backstory is a late-game revelation — it should be seeded early (his fighting ability, his controlled rage, his instinct to protect through violence, Jaehyun's cryptic awareness of him) and revealed in full later
  • Jaehyun likely knows Jungwon's real past — this is part of why he both respects and fears Jungwon's involvement with Solar
  • The reveal recontextualizes everything: his protectiveness isn't just romantic — it's the behavior of someone who has lived in danger and knows exactly what it looks like

SOLAR — FLASHBACK ARCHITECTURE

For scattering past moments through present narrative:

Tier 1 — Early fragments (age 12–13): The headset. Scabbed knees. Ranked queue running while homework sat unfinished. Warm, nostalgic — the before. Used early in the rewrite to establish who she was before everything.

Tier 2 — The HYBE audition (age 15): Trembling fingers on a mic. The moment she chose the idol path. Ambivalent — she wanted it and was terrified.

Tier 3 — The Jihoon years (age 15–17): Darkest material. Multiple parties, not one. Should feel like a slow tide pulling her under — mundane cruelty, not dramatic cruelty. The worst party (the photo) should land like a gut punch, not a horror set piece. These fragments escalate in intensity as Jihoon escalates in the present.

Tier 4 — Le Sserafim trainee period (age 17–18): Meeting the girls for the first time as a trainee. The new support system forming. The beginning of her learning what it feels like to be safe. These are recovery fragments.

Tier 5 — The dorm nights (age 19): Grinding ranked alone while members slept. The reclamation. The gaming chair in the dark as defiance.

Rule: Each tier of flashbacks escalates as Jihoon/Danielle escalate in present time. The reader pieces her together as she's being taken apart.


OPEN QUESTIONS — DECISIONS NEEDED BEFORE WRITING

  • Solar's secret in-game name — What did she play under before being revealed? Should be something untraceable but potentially meaningful in hindsight.
  • The Shock contract specifics — How exactly does HYBE greenlight it? Was it secret first, then discovered? Or negotiated from the start? This affects the early chapters' tension level significantly.
  • Chaerin's specific trigger for her spiral — What starts it? Matters for how authentically it's written.
  • Chaerin's substance — Needs to be specific enough to write the spiral accurately.
  • Did Chaerin know what happened to Solar at the worst party? If yes, when did she find out, and how does she carry that guilt? This is potentially a major emotional scene.
  • Did Jaehyun know in real time about Solar and Jihoon, or did he piece it together after? Changes every scene between them.
  • Who helped Jungwon escape The Circle? This character may be worth introducing or referencing.
  • Solar's real name — "Choi So-young" was referenced in prior planning notes. Confirm or change.
  • The Le Sserafim trainee meeting — When does Solar first meet each member as a trainee? Are there any notable first impressions worth building into flashback scenes?
