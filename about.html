on the name solar

I always liked names that felt celestial — something that sounded like it existed slightly outside of ordinary things. Solar fit that. But the reason it stuck goes deeper than the sound of it.

When I first made Solar in Delicate, I was in a really rough, dark place. That period carries through the novel — it's not exactly a light read, and it wasn't meant to be. But Solar, as a character and as a name, became a source of brightness for me during a time that was otherwise pretty lonely. She became a reason to remember that things could always be worse, technically.

She symbolizes, more than anything, what it means to focus your energy toward something that gives off light rather than takes it.

The name holds a genuinely special place in my heart now, because of what it got me through.

how the worlds came to be

★ the worlds ★

Delicate

It was originally meant to be a lighthearted crack-fic. But my life had other plans, and writing became my only outlet. The story turned into something much darker and deeper than it was ever planned to be. On the outside it may seem like a mess of plotlines and characters — but every minute detail served a greater purpose for me and my mental health at the time. Every word came from a place I had nowhere else to put.

Ending it took a very long time. I originally said 80 chapters, then 100, then another 80. None of my friends wanted to read it because it's "too long." Every six months I come back to it — seemingly when things feel heavy again — and it feels electric every time. I carry the attitudes of some of its characters with me every single day.

Hoax

My first real SMAU attempt. I'd become obsessed with the format — the way it mixes lighthearted group chat chaos with genuinely serious written scenes. I had just become an ENHYPEN fan and thought, what better way to try this? I wanted something different from Delicate. Funny, bright, something my friends might actually read. (They didn't, not really.) But writing Hoax became genuinely fun because it let me pour a lot of my own friendships into it. Jihoon and Cherry's dynamic feels so real it's like watching a bunch of dumb best friends in real time. Loud until it isn't.

Runners

A combination of everything I find most compelling in my other works — the sensuality of Delicate, the SMAU elements of Hoax, the gaming world of Petal & Thorn, all thrown together. The last time I wrote a college AU was First Love, which is not something I like to look back on. Runners is my coming-of-age, modernized take on that space. Grown up, more layered, and a lot more me.

Havenbrook

A darker take — pulling from the themes of GO (matured) and the gang elements of Recherche and Delicate. Not every person who participates in something dark is inherently bad. Haechan in this world is genuinely good. He still helps people, still wants to keep them safe. It's about two people who love each other but know they can't commit — because one of them is meant for something bigger than a town being slowly consumed by darkness.

♡ 2,388 notes

Casual

Much lighter than Havenbrook. Think of it as a less complex Runners — Haechan isn't a bad boy here at all. Casual is based on the Chappell Roan song of the same name, which might give some context. It's about returning to a place you never fully left, and the different textures that come with that.

Secret

Secret pulls from real themes still very much present in our world — in that sense it might actually be the darkest work in the archive despite its surface. WLW is different in flavor and feeling from everything else I write. Soorin and Minji are so specific in who they are that I'm genuinely afraid to write them wrong. I wanted to write it because it's a part of myself I want to tap into more and be truer to. It's special because it's not like any of the others.
